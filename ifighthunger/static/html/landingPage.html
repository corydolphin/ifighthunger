When you play Santa, we'll play Santa.

Earn a chef's meal when you volunteer.

On December 1st, take our pledge to fight holiday hunger.

We launch December 1st

I FIGHT HUNGER inspires the eradication of holiday hunger. Take our pledge to serve your community, no matter how small your effort might be.  Our plan: Promote an advent calendar to fight hunger.

Starting December 1st, every day until Christmas we feature a new restaurant partner who incentivizes daily social action so that volunteers will step forward to help their community.  Instead of a tiny piece of chocolate, we’d like to recognize your service to our community with a full fledged private dinner for two with one of our top chefs.  All you have to do is submit a photo or video of you in action, in your community, and we’ll see to it that you have an opportunity to be honored.  (We can’t honor your service if you don’t step forward, so please connect with us!)

I FIGHT HUNGER was created on November 16th, 2012 after filming a video for KitchenlessChef.com, my culinary video blog.  I realized how fortunate I have been to experience some of the world’s finest culinary creations, and that privilege inspired me to make an effort to prevent holiday hunger.  After seeing poverty first hand in the Philippines and in Laos, it’s time the Kitchenless Chef platform evolves to support our community outside the kitchen.  I believe this is the tip of a larger project, but for now it is highly relevant at a time of year when the holiday spirit inspires us to be a bit more aware of the people around us.

Our goal is to connect top chefs with volunteers who are making an effort to give back to the community.  Anyone can enter, showcase their effort, and be recognized. It is our hope that recognizing your efforts, and your story, will inspire others to do the same.  With the platform we’re creating, we aim to incentivize community activism and connect folks for the holiday season to do a more effective job of recognizing and combating hunger in our nation.  Our restaurant partners will start first in Washington, DC, which has the highest percentage of food stamp usage than any other city in our nation.  Once we’ve proven the concept here, we’ll look to expand into other major metropolitan areas.

On Christmas eve, we’ll feature Carmine’s restaurant who is recognizing both a volunteer (and guest) and a less fortunate family of six to share in a feast on Christmas day. Plus, on Christmas day, we’ll feature multiple volunteer opportunities for you to earn recognition from several restaurants including a top chef’s menu with pairings (a $600+ meal of a lifetime – just pay the tax and gratuity).

This season is full of opportunities to serve our community– and the holiday spirit will be brighter if we all proclaim, “I FIGHT HUNGER!”

Semper Fidelis,
